Derrick Henry. The 2021 MVP that never was.

Author

Derrick Henry could have (and should have) won the NFL MVP in 2021 if it were not for breaking his fifth metatarsal in his foot, which is known as a Jones fracture. He would miss 10 weeks of the regular season and the MVP out of sight.

Imagine if you will. You are a Tennessean living in Nebraska and you hail from the Country Music Capital of the World and you’re favorite professional sports team is actually having a good year! Most of the teams in the state are having a good year for once which is a nice change. You are taking some sports journalism classes and you have been tasked with writing your first big piece for the sports paper and you figure, “Hey, my Tennessee Titans are pretty hot right now and my favorite player The King, El Tractorcito, number 22 is absolutely demolishing the rushing charts. How about I write the story of the MVP lock that is Derick Henry?” Well, that would be me, exactly four days before those hopes and dreams of a Titans MVP and potential Superbowl went down the drain after Derrick Henry was injured and ruled out for multiple weeks after a match up against the conference rivals (if you can call it that) Houston Texans.’

In a “broadcasters jinx” style of unfortunate circumstances that I still am convinced that I caused, we will cover the unfortunate story of the NFL MVP campaign that never was. This is how Derrick Henry was shorted the single season rushing record and how he compares to other running back MVPS in the 21st century

So, through the eight games that Derrick Henry played in the Titans regular season, he put up 987 yards through the first eight games. And despite played the least amount of games in the best group of rushers in the NFL, Henry still ended up just outside the top 10 rushers for the 2021 season.

Looking at the other running backs in the NFL and the incredible season Johnathon Taylor had, Derrick Henry was in a really good group of athletes to compete against. And in the NFL anything can happen, as seen with Henry’s unfortunate injury in week 8 versus Houston. Henry could have gained more than expected, he could have gained less. We just will never know what could have been.

A common thing to help compare potential MVP material is to look to the past. Most MVPs are typically, especially in recent years, Quarterbacks. There hasn’t been a non-quarterback MVP since 2009 when Adrian Peterson had a breakout season as the Minnesota Vikings star running back. There have only been FOUR running back MVPS since 2000, so let’s look at how Derrick Henry compares in yards gained versus the former MVP running backs of the 21st century including his 2020 season.

With 937 yards through week 8, The King was having the best running back season everyone has seen in a long time. The best in over a decade in my opinion. Still through the 2022 season, Henry has SIX career games with 200+ rush yards and 2+ rushing touchdowns since 1950! Four of which are against the Houston Texans alone. He has twice as many as Hall of Famers like Barry Sanders and Jim Brown, who are not included in this post, and LaDainian Tomlinson who all have three of such games. El Tractorcito was looking to stiff arm and run over anyone who stood in his way…

Except breaking a bone in his foot.

But Derrick Henry only played 8 weeks, how can you compare less half a season to other MVP running backs? Well, lets look at how Derrick Henry’s 8 week season matches up to the MVPs. We can take Henry’s yards gained through the eight weeks and find his average yards per game was around 117.125 yards. By multiplying this over the ten games he missed, we estimate how many yards Derrick Henry could have had. Looking at the data, Derrick Henry was en route to surpass the rushing king and 80s legend Eric Dickerson.

So, will we ever know if Derrick Henry was prone to have the season the numbers, fans, and experts expected him to have? We will never know. But as a Titans fan who has been let down time and time again in the playoffs, I still like to believe that The King still reigns as one of the best running backs to have played the game. He still has many, many years to prove this in the history books, so just watch out for Derrick Henry to break the record that Eric Dickerson set over 35 years ago.
